Your first visit

What Sunday morning looks like

There is no wrong way to arrive. Here is the shape of the morning, start to finish.

1

Arrive

Come anytime between 8:15 and 9:30. Parking is free in the lot, and someone near the door will be glad to point you in.

2

Orthros

Morning prayers begin at 8:15. Feel free to arrive partway through — many parishioners do.

3

Divine Liturgy

Our main service begins at 9:30. Stand, sit, or simply take it in — there is no expectation either way.

4

Social Hour

After the service, join us downstairs for coffee and conversation. This is the easiest place to say hello.

What you'll see

A few things you might notice

Orthodox worship engages all the senses. None of it requires participation — only your presence.

Icons

Images of Christ and the saints line the walls. You are welcome to look, but never expected to do anything more.

Incense

A sweet, smoky scent fills the church during parts of the service. It is part of how we worship, not a distraction from it.

Candles

Many people light a candle near the entrance as a quiet prayer. Feel free to light one, no instructions needed.

Sign of the Cross

You will see people cross themselves at different moments. It is personal, not required, and easy to simply observe.

Frequently Asked Questions

If your question is not here, reach out — we would rather you ask than wonder.

Can I visit if I'm not Orthodox?
Absolutely. Our doors are open to everyone, and no one is turned away. Many of our parishioners came to the Orthodox Church later in life, from many different backgrounds. Someone from our welcome team is always glad to meet you.
Can I bring my kids?
Please do. Children are part of our worship, not separate from it — there is no children's service to step out for. If you need a quieter moment, a nursery and quiet space are available for young children and nursing parents.
How will I know what to do?
You won't be expected to know anything. Stand, sit, and follow along as much or as little as feels comfortable. No one will ask you to participate in something you're unsure about.
What should I wear?
Come as you are comfortable. Most parishioners dress in smart-casual attire — slacks, skirts, blazers — but you will never be turned away for what you're wearing.
Can I receive communion?
Holy Communion is reserved for baptized and prepared Orthodox Christians. Visitors are warmly invited to come forward during that part of the service to receive a blessing and a small piece of blessed bread.
How long is the service?
Sunday Divine Liturgy runs about an hour and a half, from 9:30 to roughly 11:00. You're welcome to arrive partway through if that fits your morning better.

We would love to meet you

Join us this Sunday, or reach out first if you have a question. Either way, we're glad you found us.
